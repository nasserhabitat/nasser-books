Book Description

Tasbeeh (glorification) and Sujud (prostration) are the two wings of servitude in Islam. This book explores them not as mere rituals, but as a comprehensive way of life that integrates heartfelt belief, verbal remembrance, and practical action. It demonstrates how glorification purifies the heart through awareness of God's perfection, while prostration serves as the ultimate physical expression of humility and submission.

Using a "Qur'anic Linguistics" approach, the book deconstructs the linguistic and symbolic meanings of these concepts to reveal a profound connection between inner sanctification and practical, righteous living. It presents a new interpretation of prostration as an existential project that shapes human identity, connects us to the universe, and directs us toward psychological and social reform in the modern era.

Key Features:

  • Redefines glorification and prostration as an integrated way of life.
  • Links sacred rituals to the challenges and realities of daily living.
  • Introduces a "Qur'anic Linguistics" methodology for deeper understanding.
  • Analyzes key themes such as the story of Adam, the Hajj, and cosmic submission.
  • Offers a framework for psychological and social reform through spiritual practice.

Chapter Highlights

Conceptual Foundations

This section lays the groundwork by exploring the essential meaning of glorification as the transcendence of God from all imperfection, and prostration as the ultimate practical manifestation of submission.

The Command to Prostrate to Adam

A deep dive into the story of Adam's creation, analyzing the command to prostrate as a test of obedience that reveals the nature of pride versus true submission to God's will.

Hajj: A Journey of Praise and Prostration

Frames the pilgrimage to Mecca as a comprehensive journey that combines glorification and prostration, leading to self-discovery, knowledge-building, and spiritual renewal.

Prostration in Surat Al-Hajj

Examines how the entire universe—from the heavens to the earth—submits to God's divine plan, contrasting traditional interpretations with a symbolic reading of cosmic prostration.

The Book's Objective

This work is not merely an academic study; it is an invitation to live glorification and prostration as a unified way of life that connects the believer to God, the universe, and oneself. In a world of increasing challenges, it offers timeless tools for achieving spiritual balance, humility, and purpose, paving the way for success in this life and the hereafter.
