About This Book

This groundbreaking work reveals the Uthmanic script as not merely a calligraphic tradition, but as a sophisticated linguistic system encoding profound Quranic meanings. Dr. Nasser Ibn Dawood presents a revolutionary approach to Quranic studies through meticulous analysis of early Islamic manuscripts.

Key Discoveries:

  • Script as Semantic Code: Demonstrates how orthographic variations in the Uthmanic script preserve subtle linguistic distinctions
  • Digital Paleography: Applies cutting-edge digital analysis to ancient Quranic manuscripts
  • Contextual Linguistics: Reveals how script variations relate to thematic contexts within the Quran
  • Historical Accuracy: Corrects common misconceptions about Quranic orthography

Methodological Innovation

The book introduces a novel analytical framework combining:

  • Computational analysis of early Quranic codices
  • Comparative study of regional script traditions
  • Linguistic anthropology of early Islamic society
  • Theological implications of orthographic choices

Practical Applications

This research provides essential tools for:

  • Quranic textual criticism
  • Historical linguistics of Arabic
  • Islamic manuscript studies
  • Contemporary Quranic interpretation

Special Features

The book includes:

  • High-resolution reproductions of key manuscript pages
  • Comparative tables of script variations
  • Case studies of significant textual variants
  • Practical exercises for students of Quranic sciences
