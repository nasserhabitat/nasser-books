Preface

The Code of the Qur’an: An Operating Manual for a New Age explores how the Qur’an functions as a living system — a divine architecture of consciousness, meaning, and creation. It invites modern readers to move from recitation to activation, from memorization to manifestation.

The book approaches the Qur’an as a self-operating system governed by the law of correspondence (al-iqtirān), where language and existence mirror one another. It bridges revelation and reality, offering tools for decoding divine meaning through linguistic, symbolic, and systemic analysis.

About the Author

Nasser Ibn Dawood is an engineer and Qur’anic researcher specializing in systemic linguistics and philosophical tafsīr. His work integrates classical revelation studies with modern systems thinking, digital semiotics, and artificial intelligence ethics.
